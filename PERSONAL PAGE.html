Just check off a few dozen adjectives and you can get a mini-novel. It looks good, but are personality tests are really as comprehensive or accurate as it looks? In my opinion there’s a 60% chance that its accurate. One can’t judge a person’s character based on a test but on a person’s action and their determination to push forward even after they have come to a standstill in life statistics is deaf, dumb, and blind. It measures associations, but only under the right conditions. For example, data sets smaller than 25 people are filled with too much individual information and not enough group information. Trustworthy statistics needs large numbers of people to be accurate. before you can trust a personality test , you have to clearly define what you want to predict; identify the personality factor that causes it and find a test developed specifically to measure that factor.
